A lot of my favourite albums were released in the latter half of the 2000s, and fun.'s debut _Aim and Ignite_ is definitely one of them. I would like to thank my cousin Laurie for introducing me to this one ( she had it saved on her iPod, so I found it when listening to _Some Nights_). 

_Aim and Ignite_ sounds like the less refined, more alternative younger brother of _Some Nights_. It also is a bit like if _Pretty.Odd._ grew up and got off the drugs to face reality, while maintaining the whimsy. (which may likely very well have inspired the album, given that both bands are fans of each other, to the point of collaboration on the song _C'mon_) This similarity is very obvious in the music, probably due to all the strings and Andrew Dost's trumpet, making it an ideal lazy Sunday album.

The album opens with _Be Calm_, and fun. really throws everything they've got into it. It's a very interesting song, with the protagonist alternating between getting worked up and convinving himself to calm down, with the music following suit. It also has one of the most emo lines ever ("You hate your pulse because it thinks you're still alive") and goes into an absolute belter of a bridge. This is followed by _Benson Hedges_, opening with an acapella intro and then barely ever slowing down. I have no idea why it's a play on words of a cigarette company. Maybe it's an inside joke or something. 

_All The Pretty Girls_ is a party-esque song, but because it's by fun. it's the Party Song of Regret and Angst. It has a drum beat that's very _That Thing You Do!_ style, and Jack Antonoff going wild on guitar in the bridge, so yeah. fun. 

_I Wanna Be The One_ is a very planitive, beautiful love song (be it romatic or platonic) with the protagonist singing about how he wants to put his love interest's accomplishments into a song, a bit similar to Tally Hall's _The Whole World and You_. I think that's a Glockenspiel in the bridge. Again, the arrangements on this album are nothing short of sublime, and that is very evident on this track. This is then followed by the sarcastic-drunken (except he's not drinking anymore) _At Least I'm Not As Sad (As I Used To Be)_. You can really see where the band gets its name from here, because it sounds like everyone playing and singing on this track is just having a helluva lot of, well, fun.

_Light a Roman Candle With Me_ is a very lazy day sort of song, backed by piano and strings, and the album title finally appears here, though in a very fleeting line. Then we have _Walking the Dog_ (memorably labelled incorrectly on one YouTube video as "fun. by fun." causing a lot of confusion for a lot of us (shout out to Kavinka)). The bridge also references Don Henley's hit _The Boys of Summer_.

_Barlights_ is one of the two songs on this album that gets a reference in _Stars_ on _Some Nights_. The gospel choir comes back for this one. Nate Ruess also is possibly dissing his bandmates here with the "One of us sings, and one of us drinks, and one of us is nothing at all" (ouch).

Right before the album closes, Nate Ruess decides to punch us in the gut with _The Gambler_. It's such a raw, heartfelt, emotional love song, and it's all the more impactful when you also know it's his parents he's singing about, from the perspective of his mother. It's just Nate singing, backed by piano, strings and trumpet, but it's the kind of love song the Beatles could only dream of writing (well maybe _In My Life_ or _Here, There, and Everywhere_ gets close).

The band all gets together for the 7 minute rambling closer, _Take Your Time (Coming Home)_, in which Nate defends his choice of becoming a musician to everyone back home. It sounds like a pub singalong or jam session, in the best way possible.

Maybe there was something in the air at that point in time, for these bands to make records of Whimsy and Joy. Maybe there's still bands out there making them, but they never get as famous as fun. Either way, if y'all do know anymore, please let me know. Thanks.
