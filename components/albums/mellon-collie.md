Mellon Collie and the Infinite Sadness is a grand, elaborate double album combining orchestral rock with classic 90s American grunge. With 28 tracks and a running time of over 2 hours, it has rightfully earned its place as one of the most ambitious grunge albums ever recorded. 

Atmospherically (and perhaps thematically as well) the album resembles a house party in 90s U.S. Suburbia. Ranging from the lullaby-esque *Take Me Down* to the grand opener *Tonight, Tonight*, which features the Chicago Symphony Orchestra, to the absolutely fuzzy pre-screamo *Tales of a Scorched Earth*, this is the Smashing Pumpkins showcasing literally everything they have (and the result is pretty impressive). 

The album also spawned a slew of radio hits, the most famous of which is arguably *1979*, which paired a ballad-y melody with an uncharacteristic (for the Pumpkins, at the time) 808 drum loop, and marked the shift of their sound to a more synth-rock based tone. Aside from that, *Bullet with Butterfly Wings* (the inspiration for countless "rat in a cage" memes), the distorted, nihilistic *Zero*, the country-esque acoustic *Thirty-Three*, and the aforementioned *Tonight, Tonight* were all released as singles, to varying degrees of critical and commercial success.

But this album really shines in the lesser played songs. The 9-minute epic *Porcelina of the Vast Oceans*, the nostalgia-fuelled *Galapagos*, the initially endearing, and subsequently worrying *Lily (My One and Only)*, the beatboxed-looped *Beautiful*, every song on the album is memorable for different reasons. While this might cause it to lose a bit of cohesiveness (a Herculean task regardless when you have 28 songs to work with), the album still works as a collection of thematically similar songs.
