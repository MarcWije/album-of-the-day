commonly regarded as the defining album of the shoegaze genre (so-called because band members would perform on stage while gazing at their guitar pedals), loveless took years to complete and cost so much money that My Bloody Valentine was subsequently dropped by their label, Creation.

the word dreamscape comes to mind when describing this album, the vocals saturated and, in some cases, drowned in layers of distorted guitars, synths, and myriad effects piled up on top of each other to the extent that you're never entirely certain what's going on at the bottom. It's the sonic equivalent of being backstage at a show, with the haze machine on full and dim ambient lighting. 

The opener, "only shallow" sets the tone for the album, literally, with its screaming guitars drawling past their rhythm, and the barely audible vocals in the background, like just another instrument. It's hard to understand how songs that are so textured and atmospheric can also be groovy and catchy, but My Bloody Valentine has achieved that with songs like "come in alone", "soon", and "when you sleep".

"sometimes", arguably the most radio-friendly, ballady track on the album, in my opinion is nonetheless a wall of angsty sound washed over by infinite fuzz. 

"turn my head into sound" sings Kevin Shield, and that's more or less what this album does.
