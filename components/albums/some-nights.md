When I was 12, fun. released Some Nights, an album that ended up shaping my music taste for good. Despite having released their (in hindsight, arguably even better) debut album _Aim and Ignite_ back in 2009, they burst into public consciousness fuelled by a hat-trick of chart topping singles, led by the Grammy winning _We Are Young_, followed by the title track, and _Carry On_. 

The album opens with the _Some Nights Intro_ (a very original title) which, despite its name, is much more than merely an instrumental intro or audio clip. It gives us a glimpse of the kind of rich orchestration and polish that the album has, not to mention the slightly theatrical air, Nate Ruess' impressive vocal range, and the wry cynicism of the lyrics across the whole album. It makes you feel like you've walked into a concert, a theme that carries over into the title track _Some Nights_. Starting with an acapella intro that choir kids would attempt to replicate for years to come ( I speak from firsthand experience) it adds in piano, drums and a beautiful guitar riff over the chorus (which also may have been attempted by many guitarists, myself definitely included). The music video to the song is set during the U.S.  Civil War, and the drums sound like they fit that theme perfectly. 

Next up is _We Are Young_, a change of tone entirely. The track features Janelle Monae as a guest vocalist, has a music video depicting a slow motion bar fight, and was described by TIME magazine as an "energy drink cocktail of a single" (or something along those lines). It is a testament to the song's universal appeal that a bunch of 12 year olds who had never even seen the inside of a bar adopted it as their anthem (to be fair, the title was an apt description). The album continues with their third single, Carry On, which was a pretty inspiring anthem. It adds to the vibe of the album being support media for someone having a midlife crisis in their late twenties. This atmosphere continues with _It Gets Better_ , a manic electronicky pop song that sounds like Nate is trying to convince himself of the title, but in the most fun. (geddit) way possible. 

_Why Am I The One_ is one of the most mature, and very much American songs on the album. Ending with snippets from some nights, and a bit of a teaser for Stars, it actually works to transition into a lot of other songs, by fun. or others, including the next track _All Alone_, another delusional song where Nate sings about finding a wind up doll as a replacement for his ex girlfriend. It's very whimsically depressing. This is then followed by _All Alright_, which is the musical equivalent of a man down on his luck, walking down the street kicking a stone in front of him. 

_One Foot_ is a return back to the energy of the first few tracks, except this time it's to rage against mainstream American (one presumes) Christianity in general, and additionally their treatment of the LGBT community (again, presumably). The track also has really cool drums that build up perfectly to the next track:

_Stars_, opening with a burst of drums and the slowed down guitar riff and vocals from the title track. It is an absolutely brilliant reworking of the music into yet another existential song. ("Some Nights I rule the world, with Barlights and Pretty girls" is a genius lyric when you know that Barlights and All the Pretty Girls are tracks on fun.'s first album). The second section of the song goes on for quite a while, clocking in at almost 7 minutes in total, and while it isn't as genuinely fun as _Aim and Ignite's_ closing track _Take Your Time (Coming Home)_, it's still a fitting way to end the album. 

Unless you have the Deluxe version, in which case you also get to listen to _Out On The Town_, a bonus track that is one of my personal favourites of theirs. The bridge with Andrew Dost's muted trumpet ("and I know I could be more clever, and I know I could be more strong") is genuinely tear-inducing. It is a rare example of a bonus track that also works as an additional closer, and not just a fun addition. 

Perhaps it's better than fun. never reunited after their extended hiatus. Maybe Jack's too busy running around with Taylor Swift, maybe Nate Ruess is having too much fun with _The Format_ (whose new album is admittedly a banger). Maybe leaving at just the two albums ensures that these two will be fondly remembered as classics of the late 2000s/early 2010s rather than forgotten after they lose relevance. Or maybe we really do need another fun. album. Either way, this one is still there to listen to, so please do.
